10 Essential French Grammar Rules

1. Gender of Nouns & Article Agreement

Most fundamental concept affecting multiple aspects of French

Every noun is either masculine (le) or feminine (la)
Articles must match: le garçon (the boy) vs. la fille (the girl)
Indefinite articles: un garçon (a boy) vs. une fille (a girl)
Plural for both: les/des
Common pattern hints:
Most nouns ending in -e are feminine
Most nouns ending in consonants are masculine

2. Subject Pronouns & Basic Verb Agreement

Essential for forming any complete sentence

je (I), tu (you informal)
il/elle (he/she)
nous (we)
vous (you formal/plural)
ils/elles (they)
Key rule: Always use subject pronouns with verbs
Example: Je parle français (I speak French)

3. Present Tense Regular Verb Conjugation

Foundation for expressing basic actions

-er verbs (most common):
je parle, tu parles, il/elle parle
nous parlons, vous parlez, ils/elles parlent

-ir verbs:
je finis, tu finis, il/elle finit
nous finissons, vous finissez, ils/elles finissent

4. Essential Irregular Verbs

Critical high-frequency verbs needed for basic communication

être (to be):
je suis, tu es, il/elle est
nous sommes, vous êtes, ils/elles sont

avoir (to have):
j'ai, tu as, il/elle a
nous avons, vous avez, ils/elles ont

5. Basic Negation

How to say 'no' or 'not'
Structure: ne + verb + pas

Examples:
Je ne parle pas anglais (I don't speak English)
Ce n'est pas difficile (It's not difficult)

6. Question Formation

Three main ways to ask questions

Intonation: Tu parles français?
Est-ce que: Est-ce que tu parles français?
Inversion: Parles-tu français?

7. Adjective Agreement

How adjectives change to match nouns
Basic rules:

Add -e for feminine
Add -s for plural

Example:
petit garçon → petite fille
petits garçons → petites filles

8. Possessive Adjectives

Showing ownership

Masculine:
mon, ton, son (my, your, his/her)

Feminine:
ma, ta, sa

Plural:
mes, tes, ses

9. Basic Prepositions & Contractions

Essential for showing relationships between words

à + le = au
de + le = du
à + les = aux
de + les = des

Examples:
Je vais au restaurant (I'm going to the restaurant)
La porte du café (The door of the café)

10. Tu vs. Vous (Formal/Informal)

Critical for social appropriateness

Tu:
Informal situations
Friends, family, children

Vous:
Formal situations
Strangers, authority figures
Multiple people (always)
